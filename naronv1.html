Case Choice

I will discuss my new computer build as that has been on my mind lately as I have just finished for the most part. I started with the basics of a Corsair Carbide Air Series 540. This allowed me to have a lot of room and keep critical components such as my video card and motherboard in a seperate space from my hard drives, power supply unit, dvd writer and most cabling. This case also features "hot swappable" hard drive bays in the bottom to do any later additions or to work on hard drives for other units. See below for pictures.

Case Pics

This is the front view of my case, Notice how the front metal grid has really nice airflow to the left-most chamber. This is the chamber that will accept the Motherboard, CPU, Graphics card (GPU) and the memory chips (RAM). These elements create most of the extreme heat that you will witness when running high performance gaming or computing. This is the maine rason that I chose this particular case.

In this image you will see the motherboard side of the case. You can see the dual fans that go behind the metal grill I mentioned above. This will bring air into the case and allow for extra air cooling. There is also a fan at the rear of the case that will act as the exhaust. The case also makes a really nice presentation as you will notice the large rubber grommets that will allow you to route the cabling through them with limited exposure to the awesome "window" into the side of the machine.
